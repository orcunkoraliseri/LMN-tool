# LMN Tool — Graphical Abstract (Image Prompt)

> **Purpose:** This document contains a detailed image generation prompt for the LMN Tool graphical abstract.
> The abstract communicates the tool's **Simplistic Modular Holistic (SMH)** methodology through a layered,
> isometric urban visualisation suitable for journal figures, conference posters, and repository headers.

---

## Concept Overview

The graphical abstract should read **top-to-bottom** (or left-to-right) as a progressive stack of thematic
layers, each building on the previous one — mirroring the exact workflow of the tool. The final visual
impression is a **complete positive-energy district (PED)** that has been co-designed across spatial,
energy, mobility, and green dimensions.

---

## Image Generation Prompt

**Style:** Scientific graphical abstract. Flat-design isometric illustration. Clean white or very light grey background.
Bold, modern sans-serif typography (similar to Inter or Outfit). Vibrant but harmonious colour palette — one
distinct accent colour per layer (see below). No photorealism; use a polished, diagrammatic illustration style
with thin outlines and subtle drop shadows. Think "Nature Energy" or "Joule" journal graphical abstract aesthetic.

---

**Overall layout:** Vertical stack of five horizontal bands, each representing one layer (Layer 0 through Layer 4),
connected by downward-pointing arrows between bands to show the sequential progression. Each band occupies roughly
equal vertical space. The right side of each band carries a short label. Below the final band, a single large
"output" panel shows the complete PED profile.

---

### Band 0 — Entry Point

**Accent colour:** Neutral dark charcoal (#1E1E2E)

**Visual content:**
- A minimalist question mark icon or chat bubble at the centre
- Two rounded-rectangle option cards side by side:
  - "Existing Neighbourhood" (greyed out / inactive)
  - "New Neighbourhood" (highlighted, with a glowing outline in the accent colour)
- A "Get Started →" button below the cards
- **Label (right side):** `LAYER 0 — Entry Point`

---

### Band 1 — Neighbourhood Selection

**Accent colour:** Deep blue-violet (#4A5BFF)

**Visual content:**
- A small isometric city block (bare geometry — buildings without systems) at the centre-left
- Five horizontal icon rows, each representing one parameter with a small pictogram and a label:
  1. 🏘 **Land Use** — Residential / Commercial / Mixed-Use / Industrial
  2. 🌆 **Context** — Urban / Suburban / Rural
  3. 📐 **Density** — High / Medium / Low
  4. 🔲 **Layout** — Grid / Curvilinear / Superblock
  5. 🏗 **Envelope** — Standard / High-Performance Construction
- A small "matched neighbourhood card" output thumbnail (isometric block with area + building count)
- **Label (right side):** `LAYER 1 — Neighbourhood Selection`

---

### Band 2 — Energy

**Accent colour:** Amber / Solar yellow (#FFC857)

**Visual content:**
- Same isometric city block, now with rooftop solar panels (PV arrays) rendered in amber, and wind turbine icons
- Icons or badges floating above rooftops for: PV Roof, PV Facade, PV-T, STC, Wind, Geothermal, Biomass
- A small energy bar chart (demand vs. generation) on the right of the block
- A mini treemap thumbnail labelled "Energy Demand Breakdown" and a mini line-chart labelled "PV Generation Profile"
- **Label (right side):** `LAYER 2 — Energy`

---

### Band 3 — Mobility

**Accent colour:** Electric teal (#00C9A7)

**Visual content:**
- Same isometric city block with roads highlighted; EV charging station icons on streets
- Icons or badges for: EV, EV Public Transport, EV Charging Stations, V2G Stations, Bicycle lanes, Pedestrian paths
- A small bar or donut chart showing "EV Load" vs "V2G Feed-in"
- Bicycle lane stripe along one road edge; pedestrian walkway on another
- **Label (right side):** `LAYER 3 — Mobility`

---

### Band 4 — Green Infrastructure

**Accent colour:** Leaf green (#3DDC84)

**Visual content:**
- Same isometric city block, now fully enriched:
  - Green roofs (living plant texture) on flat rooftops
  - Vertical greening on building facades
  - Tree-lined streets (linear greenery)
  - Roof gardens / food gardens on some buildings
  - PV-Green Roof integrated modules (small solar panel + plant hybrid icon)
  - Landscape PV (LPV) tiles on open ground areas
- A mini area chart labelled "LPV Annual Yield"
- **Label (right side):** `LAYER 4 — Green Infrastructure`

---

### Final Output Panel — Complete PED Profile

**Accent colour:** Multi-colour gradient blending all four layer colours (blue-violet → amber → teal → green)

**Visual content:**
- A large, fully-rendered isometric neighbourhood block combining ALL elements from Layers 1–4:
  - Buildings with PV panels, green roofs, vertical gardens
  - Roads with EV charging stations, bike lanes, V2G icons
  - Open green spaces with LPV tiles and food gardens
  - Wind turbines and geothermal wells indicated underground
- On the left: a **persistent sidebar schematic** (narrow vertical strip) showing stacked icons from all four layers,
  with small checkmarks — representing the tool's real persistent sidebar
- Centred below the block: the label **"Positive-Energy District (PED) Profile"** in bold
- A small badge or callout reading: **"SMH Methodology — Simplistic · Modular · Holistic"**

---

### Typography & Annotations

- **Title (top of image):** `LMN Tool — Neighbourhood Design Interface` in large bold sans-serif
- **Subtitle:** `A web-based decision-support tool for PED neighbourhood design`
- **Layer labels** (right-side vertical labels for each band) in small caps, accent colour matching the band
- **Arrow connectors** between bands: thin chevron arrows (▼) in mid-grey, with micro-labels: "Select → Filter → Advance"
- **Footer (bottom-right):** `Simplistic Modular Holistic (SMH) Framework` in small italic grey text

## Notes for the Designer / Generator

- The **persistent sidebar** (narrow vertical strip visible in the final output panel) is a key UX metaphor of
  the tool and should be visually present to communicate the "accumulated profile" concept.
- The **isometric city block** should remain recognisably the same base geometry across all bands — only
  the overlaid systems change, making the layering effect immediately legible.
- Each layer's accent colour should dominate its band without overwhelming the overall palette.
- Avoid overly complex 3D rendering — the clean, diagrammatic quality is more important than realism.
